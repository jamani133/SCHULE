germany has a alrge problem with immigrated children being unable to take part in normal classes
in order to combat this the german government has introduces so called "wilommensklassen" or welcoming classes
these welcoming classes are comprised of childrem with a migration background and difficulty speking german or understanding normal lessons
due to educational deficiencies.

but how effective are they really?

at a confrence for youg europeans we asked this question and got a few answers.

according to representatives at the confrerence, in the year 2016, about 60% of children in welcoming classes managed to leave these and
switch over to normal classes after 6 months, another 19% manages to learn enough to go to normal classes after 9 months.
it was not explained extensively what happens to the remaining 21% of children, it was only said that it can be hard for these children
to integrate into society.

all in all i would say welcoming classes are a good thing as long as the scheme is not taken too far.
